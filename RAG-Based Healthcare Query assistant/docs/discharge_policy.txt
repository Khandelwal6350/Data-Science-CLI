1. PURPOSE

The purpose of this Hospital Discharge Policy is to establish standardized procedures for the safe, timely, and well-coordinated discharge of patients from the hospital. The policy ensures continuity of care, proper communication with patients and caregivers, accurate medical documentation, and compliance with hospital standards and applicable healthcare regulations.

The objectives of this policy are to:

Ensure patients are discharged safely and appropriately.
Promote continuity of medical care after discharge.
Provide patients with complete discharge instructions.
Ensure accurate preparation of discharge documentation.
Improve patient satisfaction and reduce avoidable readmissions.
Maintain compliance with legal and healthcare requirements.
2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all patients discharged from the hospital, including:

Inpatients
Surgical patients
Medical patients
Maternity patients
Pediatric patients
Emergency patients requiring discharge
Patients transferred to another healthcare facility
Patients leaving against medical advice (AMA)

The policy applies to all hospital departments involved in patient discharge, including:

Treating Physicians
Nursing Department
Patient Administration
Pharmacy Department
Billing Department
Medical Records Department
Rehabilitation and Allied Health Services
3. DISCHARGE ELIGIBILITY

A patient may be discharged when the treating physician determines that:

The patient's medical condition is stable.
Hospital-based treatment is no longer required.
Necessary investigations and procedures have been completed.
Appropriate follow-up care has been arranged.
Required discharge medications have been prescribed.
The patient or caregiver understands the discharge instructions.

The final decision regarding discharge shall always be made by the treating physician.

4. DISCHARGE PROCESS

The discharge process shall include the following steps:

Medical assessment by the treating physician.
Preparation of the discharge summary.
Completion of all clinical documentation.
Review of medications and prescriptions.
Patient education regarding home care.
Completion of billing and financial clearance.
Return of hospital equipment where applicable.
Scheduling of follow-up appointments.
Delivery of all discharge documents to the patient.

Hospital staff shall coordinate to minimize unnecessary delays during discharge.

5. DISCHARGE DOCUMENTATION

Before leaving the hospital, the patient shall receive appropriate discharge documents, which may include:

Discharge Summary
Medical Diagnosis
Treatment Provided
Surgical Procedure Details (if applicable)
Medication Prescription
Laboratory and Diagnostic Reports
Follow-up Appointment Details
Diet and Lifestyle Instructions
Activity Restrictions
Medical Certificates (where applicable)
Referral Letters (if required)

Copies of all discharge documents shall be retained in the patient's medical record.

6. PATIENT EDUCATION

Before discharge, patients and caregivers shall receive clear instructions regarding post-discharge care.

Education shall include:

Medication schedule and dosage.
Wound care instructions.
Diet recommendations.
Physical activity guidelines.
Warning signs requiring immediate medical attention.
Follow-up appointments.
Management of chronic medical conditions.
Use of medical equipment at home, if applicable.

Patients shall be encouraged to ask questions before leaving the hospital.

7. DISCHARGE MEDICATIONS

The treating physician shall prescribe all necessary discharge medications.

Patients shall receive information regarding:

Medication names.
Dosage and frequency.
Duration of treatment.
Possible side effects.
Drug interactions.
Storage instructions.
Missed dose guidance.

The Pharmacy Department shall verify prescriptions before dispensing medications.

8. FINANCIAL CLEARANCE

Before discharge, patients shall complete all applicable financial formalities.

These include:

Settlement of outstanding hospital bills.
Insurance claim processing where applicable.
Verification of deposits and refunds.
Collection of payment receipts.
Final billing approval.

Emergency financial issues shall be handled according to hospital policies without compromising patient safety.

9. DISCHARGE AGAINST MEDICAL ADVICE (AMA)

Patients have the right to leave the hospital against medical advice.

In such cases:

The treating physician shall explain the potential medical risks.
The patient or authorized representative shall sign an AMA declaration.
The hospital shall document the circumstances in the medical record.
Necessary emergency medications or instructions may be provided when appropriate.

The hospital shall not be responsible for medical complications resulting from refusal of recommended treatment after AMA discharge.

10. TRANSFER TO ANOTHER HEALTHCARE FACILITY

Patients requiring specialized or continued care may be transferred to another healthcare facility.

Before transfer:

The receiving facility shall be informed.
Medical records shall accompany the patient.
Transfer arrangements shall be coordinated safely.
Appropriate transportation shall be arranged.
Patient or family consent shall be obtained whenever required.

The transfer shall prioritize patient safety and continuity of care.

11. CONFIDENTIALITY

All discharge records and patient medical information shall remain confidential.

Discharge information shall only be shared with:

The patient.
Authorized family members or legal representatives.
Treating healthcare professionals.
Insurance providers where authorized.
Government authorities when legally required.

The hospital shall protect all patient information according to applicable privacy laws and hospital confidentiality policies.

12. POLICY REVIEW

This Hospital Discharge Policy shall be reviewed annually by the Hospital Administration and Medical Services Department to ensure compliance with current healthcare regulations, accreditation standards, patient safety requirements, and operational practices.

The annual review shall include:

Evaluation of discharge procedures.
Review of patient feedback.
Assessment of readmission trends.
Updates based on changes in healthcare regulations.
Recommendations for improving discharge efficiency and patient care.

Any revisions shall be approved by Hospital Administration before implementation and communicated to all relevant departments.
