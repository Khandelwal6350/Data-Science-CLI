1. PURPOSE

The purpose of this Hospital Billing Policy is to establish standardized billing procedures for all medical services provided by the hospital. This policy ensures that all charges are calculated accurately, communicated transparently, and collected in a timely manner while maintaining compliance with applicable financial, legal, and healthcare regulations.

The objectives of this policy are to:

Ensure accurate and transparent billing for all hospital services.
Provide patients with a clear understanding of medical charges.
Maintain consistency in billing practices across all departments.
Support timely collection of payments and insurance claims.
Protect patient financial information and confidentiality.
Ensure compliance with applicable healthcare and financial regulations.
2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all patients receiving healthcare services from the hospital, including:

Inpatients
Outpatients
Emergency patients
Day-care patients
Self-paying patients
Patients covered under private or government insurance
Corporate healthcare program beneficiaries

This policy also applies to all departments involved in patient billing, including:

Finance Department
Billing Department
Cashier Services
Insurance and TPA Desk
Patient Administration
Medical Records Department
Clinical Departments responsible for service documentation

3. BILLING COMPONENTS

The final hospital bill shall include all applicable charges incurred during the patient's treatment.

Billing components may include, but are not limited to:

Registration Charges
Consultation Fees
Specialist Consultation Charges
Admission Charges
Room and Bed Charges
Intensive Care Unit (ICU) Charges
Nursing Care Charges
Laboratory Investigation Charges
Diagnostic Imaging Charges (X-ray, CT Scan, MRI, Ultrasound, etc.)
Pharmacy and Medication Charges
Surgical and Procedure Charges
Operation Theatre Charges
Anesthesia Charges
Medical Equipment and Consumable Charges
Blood Bank Charges
Physiotherapy Charges
Emergency Service Charges
Ambulance Charges
Dietary and Nutrition Charges (where applicable)
Miscellaneous Hospital Service Charges
Government taxes and statutory charges, where applicable

Only services actually provided to the patient shall be included in the final bill.

4. BILL GENERATION

The hospital shall generate a detailed and accurate bill after completion of treatment or at the time of patient discharge.

The billing process includes:

Recording all medical services provided.
Verification of departmental charges.
Review of medication and consumable usage.
Verification of insurance deductions, if applicable.
Calculation of taxes and applicable discounts.
Preparation of the final invoice.

Patients or their authorized representatives may request:

An itemized bill.
Duplicate invoices.
Payment receipts.
Billing clarification from the Finance and Billing Department.

All billing records shall be maintained according to the hospital's record retention policy.

5. PAYMENT METHODS

The hospital accepts multiple payment methods to provide convenience to patients.

Accepted payment methods include:

Cash
Debit Cards
Credit Cards
UPI Payments
Net Banking
Bank Transfers
Digital Wallets (where accepted)
Insurance Claims
Corporate Billing Arrangements
Government Health Scheme Payments

Payment receipts shall be issued immediately after successful payment.

Electronic payment records shall be securely maintained by the hospital.

6. INSURANCE BILLING

Patients covered under health insurance shall provide complete insurance information during registration or admission.

Insurance billing procedures include:

Verification of insurance eligibility and policy coverage.
Submission of required medical documents.
Prior authorization for procedures when required.
Coordination with the insurance provider or Third-Party Administrator (TPA).
Processing of cashless claims where available.
Settlement of approved insurance claims.

Patients remain financially responsible for:

Non-covered medical services.
Policy exclusions.
Deductibles and co-payments.
Charges exceeding approved insurance limits.
Services denied by the insurance provider.

Insurance approval does not guarantee payment for all hospital services.

7. ADVANCE PAYMENT

The hospital may require advance payment before admission for planned or elective procedures.

Advance payment requirements may depend on:

Estimated treatment cost.
Type of procedure.
Insurance approval status.
Hospital financial policy.

Emergency patients shall receive immediate medical care regardless of advance payment whenever medically necessary.

Additional advance deposits may be requested if treatment costs significantly exceed the original estimate.

Unused advance balances shall be adjusted against the final bill.

8. REFUND POLICY

Refunds shall be processed under appropriate circumstances including:

Duplicate payments.
Cancelled procedures or surgeries.
Excess advance deposits.
Billing errors.
Insurance overpayments.
Cancellation before admission.

Refund requests shall undergo verification by the Finance Department.

Approved refunds shall normally be processed within 7 to 14 working days, depending on the original payment method and banking procedures.

Refunds shall be issued only to the original payer or an authorized representative after identity verification.

9. BILL DISPUTES

Patients may raise billing concerns or disputes within 30 days from the invoice date.

The dispute resolution process includes:

Submission of a billing complaint.
Review of medical records and service documentation.
Verification of departmental charges.
Consultation with relevant clinical departments if necessary.
Issuance of corrected invoices where applicable.

The Finance and Billing Department shall investigate all genuine billing concerns and provide a written response within a reasonable timeframe.

10. LATE PAYMENTS

Outstanding hospital bills shall be paid within the payment period specified on the invoice.

Failure to make payment may result in:

Reminder notices.
Follow-up communication regarding outstanding balances.
Delay in the release of non-essential administrative documents, where permitted by hospital policy and applicable law.
Referral of unpaid accounts for further financial recovery procedures, if necessary.

Emergency medical care shall never be denied solely due to previous unpaid balances where prohibited by applicable laws.

11. CONFIDENTIALITY

The hospital is committed to maintaining the confidentiality of all patient financial information.

Billing records shall be:

Protected against unauthorized access.
Stored securely according to hospital information security policies.
Shared only with authorized personnel.
Released to insurance providers only with appropriate authorization.
Disclosed to government authorities only when legally required.

All employees handling financial information shall comply with hospital confidentiality policies and applicable privacy laws.

12. POLICY REVIEW

This Hospital Billing Policy shall be reviewed annually by the Finance Department and Hospital Administration to ensure compliance with current financial regulations, healthcare standards, insurance requirements, and operational practices.

The annual review shall include:

Evaluation of billing procedures.
Review of insurance claim processes.
Assessment of regulatory compliance.
Updates based on changes in healthcare laws.
Recommendations for improving billing accuracy, transparency, and patient satisfaction.

All revisions shall be approved by Hospital Administration before implementation and communicated to all relevant departments.
